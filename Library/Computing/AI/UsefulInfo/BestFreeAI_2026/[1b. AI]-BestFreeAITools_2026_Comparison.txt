| Tool | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|

Page 2: Specialized Research - Fact vs. Friction
Stop Googling. It’s 2026. If you’re manually clicking links, you’re essentially a digital caveman.
 * Perplexity AI:
   * Pros: Real-time citations for every claim. If it lies, you can see exactly which website it’s blaming.
   * Cons: The free tier limits "Pro" searches (the ones that use the better models).
 * NotebookLM:
   * Pros: It only knows what you tell it. It won't bring in outside garbage from the internet unless you ask. The "Audio Overview" feature is great for pretending you read the source material.
   * Cons: Useless if you don't have your own documents to feed it. It’s a closed-loop system.
 * Exa:
   * Pros: Finds high-quality "human-written" content that Google’s AI-infested index often buries.
   * Cons: Steeper learning curve; it’s a search engine for researchers, not a chatbot for someone looking for a recipe.
Page 3: Visual & Audio - Art for the Unskilled
Since none of you seem to have the patience to learn how to draw or play an instrument, these tools exist to fill the void.
 * Nano Banana (Gemini) vs. Ideogram:
   * Nano Banana: Wins on character consistency. You can make the same person do ten different things without them looking like a different species in every frame.
   * Ideogram: Still the king of text. If you need a sign that says "Go Away" and you want it spelled correctly, use this.
 * Suno v5 vs. ElevenLabs:
   * Suno: It generates an entire song—lyrics, melody, and vocals—in 30 seconds. Cons: You have zero control over the specific "voice" of the singer.
   * ElevenLabs: The absolute master of voice synthesis. If you want a specific accent or emotion, this is the tool. Cons: The free tier is tiny. You’ll run out of credits before you finish a single paragraph.
Page 4: Developer Tools - Coding for the Lazy
If you’re still using Notepad to code, please leave my classroom. Immediately.
 * Cursor:
   * Pros: It’s a fork of VS Code that actually understands your whole project folder. It can find bugs you didn't even know you had.
   * Cons: It’s a resource hog. If your laptop is more than two years old, it’ll sound like a jet engine taking off.
 * v0 / Bolt.new:
   * Pros: You describe a website, and it builds the code and shows you the preview instantly.
   * Cons: Great for prototypes, terrible for maintaining a real, complex application. It’s the fast-food equivalent of software engineering.
 * Google AI Studio:
   * Pros: Massive free limits for the Gemini 1.5/3.0 Pro models.
   * Cons: It’s a "developer playground," not a full IDE. It’s where you test ideas, not where you build them.
Page 5: Final Verdict & Summary
If you want the "best" experience for zero dollars:
 * Writing: Use Claude until you hit the limit, then switch to ChatGPT.
 * Research: Use Perplexity for facts and NotebookLM for your own notes.
 * Visuals: Use Gemini (Nano Banana) for quality, Ideogram for text.
 * Coding: Use Cursor (free tier) and AI Studio for heavy lifting.
