## Page 1: Conversational Masters

The "Big Four" still dominate the market for general-purpose nonsense. If you can't get a decent answer out of these, the problem is likely your prompting, not the machine.

* **ChatGPT (OpenAI):** The "all-rounder" that everyone uses. The free tier currently grants limited access to the flagship **GPT-5.3 Instant** model. It handles writing, basic coding, and brainstorming without much friction.
* **Google Gemini:** The "productivity accelerator". The free version provides full access to **Gemini 3 Flash** and includes generous integration with Google Workspace. It's particularly strong for research synthesis and multimodal tasks.
* **Claude (Anthropic):** Generally considered the most "human-sounding" of the lot. The free tier offers **Claude Sonnet 4.6**, which excels at creative writing and complex reasoning. Be warned: the message caps are notoriously strict during peak hours.
* **Mistral (Le Chat):** The underrated underdog. Its free tier is highly generous with few restrictions, making it excellent for technical drafts and structured output.

## Page 2: Specialized Research

Stop using standard search engines like it's 2010. If you're still scrolling through ten pages of ads to find a fact, you're failing this class.

* **Perplexity AI:** The gold standard for research that actually cites its sources. It's ideal for fact-checking and building reading lists without the usual AI hallucinations.
* **NotebookLM:** Developed by Google, this tool lets you upload your own documents or websites to generate summaries and podcast-style overviews. It’s essentially a personal research assistant that doesn't complain about the workload.
* **Exa:** A highly effective tool for finding specific information that traditional search engines tend to bury.

---

## Page 3: Visual and Multimedia Creation

If you want to make pretty pictures or mediocre music without paying a subscription, these are your best bets.

* **Nano Banana (via Gemini):** Google's go-to for AI-generated visuals, providing high consistency and impressive image editing within the Gemini 3 Flash model.
* **Adobe Firefly:** Offers a limited free credit system but remains one of the safest bets for copyright-compliant image generation.
* **Ideogram:** Still the leader for generating images that contain accurate text. If you need a logo that doesn't look like a stroke victim wrote it, use this.
* **Suno & ElevenLabs:** Suno remains a favorite for quick music experimentation, while ElevenLabs offers the most realistic voice synthesis with a usable free trial.

---

## Page 4: Developer and Workflow Utilities

For the students who actually want to build something instead of just talking to a chatbot.

* **Cursor:** A "genuinely unhinged" code editor that integrates AI directly into your local development environment. It makes coding feel like witchcraft.
* **Google AI Studio:** A powerhouse for "vibe coding" and testing Gemini models with a very high free rate limit for developers.
* **v0:** An intuitive tool for building web apps and UI components by just describing what you want.
* **Zapier Agents:** Allows you to build automated workflows—like summarizing emails or logging leads—without writing a single line of code.

---

## Page 5: Finishing Page

In summary, the AI landscape of 2026 is a minefield of "freemium" traps, but if you stick to **ChatGPT**, **Gemini**, and **Perplexity**, you'll cover 90% of your needs. Don't get distracted by every new shiny tool that pops up on Reddit. Most of them are just wrappers for models you can already access for free.
